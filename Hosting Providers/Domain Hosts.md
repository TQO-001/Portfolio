#### Shared Hosting

**Overview**  
This document compares shared hosting providers based on critical attributes, allowing for quick assessment of options based on storage, drive type, and domain offerings. Shared hosting is ideal for beginners or small-scale websites due to its cost-effectiveness, though it may offer limited resources for high-traffic sites.

**Attribute Notes**  
- **Shared Hosting:** All providers offer shared hosting, meaning resources are shared with other users. This can affect performance under high-traffic conditions.
- **Package Type:** Entry-level packages are cost-effective, suitable for beginners, small businesses, and personal websites. "Secondary" or "Entry" refers to basic packages, while some providers may offer "Advanced" options for more storage or features.
- **Storage Space (GB):** Storage impacts how much content (like images and files) can be hosted. Higher storage is generally preferable for websites with heavy media usage. For example, Absolute Hosting offers a modest 1GB, while Host Africa provides a more generous 20GB.
- **Hard Drive Type:** NVMe and SSD drives offer faster access speeds than HDD, enhancing page loading times. NVMe is typically the fastest.
- **Free Domain:** Some providers offer a free domain as a promotional benefit, either on certain conditions or with certain packages. This can be valuable if starting from scratch without a domain.

---

**General Recommendations**
- **For Personal or Small Business Sites:** Providers like Host King, Host Africa, and 1Grid offer solid entry packages with decent storage (5GB–20GB) and SSD storage for better performance.
- **If Prioritizing Speed:** Absolute Hosting with NVMe SSDs can offer a notable speed boost.
- **Domain Requirements:** Check free domain conditions, especially for Godaddy, which does not offer it free in the basic package.

**Attribute Notes**

**General Recommendations**

**Additional Notes for Selecting Hosting Providers**
- Evaluate **Bandwidth** if available: This can impact your site's ability to handle traffic. Check with the hosting provider if bandwidth is unlisted.
- **Customer Support** is essential, especially for beginners. Many providers offer 24/7 support, which is critical for troubleshooting.
- **Scalability** is key if you anticipate growth. Some providers offer easy upgrades to VPS or dedicated hosting plans as your site grows.
