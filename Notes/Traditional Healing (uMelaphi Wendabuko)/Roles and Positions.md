### **Consuming _Ukhamba_**

- **What is _Ukhamba_?**
    - A mixture of water and medicinal herbs, beaten into a frothy foam.
    - The foam is consumed as part of the initiation ritual.
- **Duration:** Seven consecutive days.
- **Daily Routine:**
    1. Wake up at 3:00 AM to prepare the mixture.
    2. Beat the mixture until a frothy foam forms.
    3. Consume the foam until burping occurs, which is considered a sign of acceptance by the ancestors.
- **Purpose:**
    - This act serves as a connection between the initiate and their ancestors, symbolizing the consumption of ancestral wisdom and the cleansing of the initiate's spirit.

## Dreams and Visions During Initiation

- **Guidance Through Dreams:**
    - Initiates often receive dreams and visions that provide guidance on specific medicinal knowledge and practices.
    - These dreams are considered communications from the ancestors, directing the initiate towards particular healing methods.
- **Specific Medicines Revealed:**
    1. **Ukhamba lesingunu (Black Lion _bhai_):** Associated with strength and protection, this medicine is believed to fortify the healer's spiritual defenses.
    2. **Sndawe (Njiti, iPalu _bhai_):** Linked to ancestral spirits known as _AbaSndawe_, this medicine facilitates communication with these entities.
    3. **Sthunywa (Blue Lion _bhai_ or Blue Impangele):** Connected to messenger spirits, this medicine aids in receiving and interpreting spiritual messages.
- **Order and Timing:**
    - The sequence and timing of these revelations vary for each initiate, reflecting the personalized nature of the ancestral guidance.

## Impande Stage

### **What is _Impande_?**

- **Definition:** The term "Impande" translates to "root" in Zulu, signifying a deeper connection to one's ancestral lineage and the foundational aspects of traditional healing.
- **Significance:** This stage represents a deeper immersion into the healing practices and a stronger bond with the ancestral spirits.

### **Process:**

- **Regalia:** The initiate wears the _amabhai_ associated with the _ukhamba_ stage, symbolizing their progress and accumulated wisdom.
- **Rituals:**
    - The specific rituals of this stage are considered sacred and are not publicly disclosed.
    - Involves the use of a personal sheep, which plays a role in the ritualistic practices.
- **Duration:** Typically lasts for two days.

---

## Final Stage: Phothularing

### **Definition:**

- **Phothularing:** This term refers to the process of establishing one's own _sgodlo_ (sacred healing space), marking the transition from apprentice to practicing healer.

### **Role of Dreams:**

- **Continued Guidance:** Even after establishing their practice, healers rely on dreams for ongoing instruction and spiritual insights.
- **Ancestral Communication:** Dreams serve as a primary medium through which ancestors communicate directives, teachings, and warnings to the healer.

### **Significance:**

- **Culmination of Training:** This stage signifies the culmination of the initiate's training and their readiness to serve the community independently.
- **Community Role:** The healer now assumes full responsibility for diagnosing and treating ailments, conducting rituals, and providing spiritual guidance.

## Post-Initiation Responsibilities

- **Community Service:** Healers play a vital role in their communities, offering services that include physical healing, spiritual counseling, and mediation between the living and the ancestors.
- **Cultural Preservation:** As custodians of traditional knowledge, healers are responsible for preserving and transmitting cultural practices and beliefs.
- **Ethical Conduct:** Healers are expected to adhere to ethical guidelines, maintaining the trust and respect of the community.

## Roles

### **Sangoma**

- **Function:** Diagnose problems and provide solutions using _ishoba_ (cow tail stick) and other spiritual tools.
- **Specialty:** Identifying root causes of ailments and spiritual disturbances.

### **Nyanga**

- **Function:** Similar to _sangomas_ but rely on candles for spiritual messages.
- **Specialty:** Combining herbal medicine and spiritual guidance to treat ailments.

### **uMthandazi**

- **Function:** Use prayer and water as primary tools for healing.
- **Specialty:**
    - Receive instructions through dreams regarding attire (_jazi_).
    - Use water from rivers, oceans, or dams for spiritual cleansing and healing.
    - Employ specific colored candles depending on the situation:
        - **Yellow:** Represents ancestors.
        - **White:** Represents the healer and their guardian angel.
        - **Blue:** Represents messengers between the healer and _Mvelinqangi_ (the Creator).
        - **Red:** Offers protection from external forces.
        - **Green:** Symbolizes growth and renewal.

### **amaGedla**

- **Function:** Provide medicine for problems without diagnosing the root cause.
- **Specialty:** Act as herbalists, offering remedies based on known ailments.

## Importance of Comprehensive Training

- **Integrated Skills:** Mastery of multiple roles enhances the healer's ability to address complex issues comprehensively.
- **Individual Strengths:** While some may excel in specific roles, comprehensive training ensures a well-rounded practice.
- **Community Impact:** Diverse skills enable healers to serve their communities more effectively, addressing both physical and spiritual needs.
